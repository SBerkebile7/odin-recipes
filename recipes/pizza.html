Description

When you use pickles with your food, you might top a burger, stuff them in a sandwich, or toss them in a salad. When you imagine a pizza, you think of gooey, delicious cheese and rich sauce, like a truffle or cream-based white sauce or a fragrant marinara, with classic toppings of cheese, meat, olives, and vegetables. What you don't think of? Putting pickles on top of that beautiful slice of pizza.

You might recoil in disgust now, but pickle pizza is quickly becoming a common sight on pizza restaurant menus. In fact, you can even order a pickle pizza straight to your door.

So where did this pickle pizza craze start? A few years ago, a pizza place called QC Pizza in Mahtomedi, Minn., created a pizza with dill pickles on it as a topping. It went viral, as crazy food combinations often do. Called the Kinda Big Dill Pizza, the pie got tons of attention, with more than 16 million views on Facebook.

The Kinda Big Dill Pizza is based on the idea of Minnesota Sushi, a snack of cream cheese spread over a dill pickle, then wrapped in ham and sliced similarly to what you'd do with a sushi roll.

    Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 prepared pizza crust
  • 1 ½ cups shredded pepper Jack cheese
  • ½ cup shredded Cheddar cheese
  • 15 hamburger dill pickle chips, or more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh dill

    Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  2. Mix oil and garlic together in a small bowl and brush over pizza crust. Pre-bake crust in the preheated oven for 2 minutes. Remove from oven.
  3. Evenly sprinkle pizza crust with pepper Jack cheese and Cheddar cheese and return to the hot oven. Bake until cheese is melted, about 2 minutes.
  4. Evenly distribute crumbled bacon, pickle slices, and crushed red pepper flakes over the crust. Return to the oven and bake for 2 more minutes. Garnish with dill.
